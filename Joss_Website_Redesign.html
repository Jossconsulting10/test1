The hidden problem

Why so many business owners get denied funding.

Lenders and banks don't deny entrepreneurs because they're unqualified. They deny them because of the small details that make a business look amateur on paper.

i

Personal email & phone number

Using a Gmail address and your cell phone signals "side hustle" — not "established business."

ii

Home address as business address

Your home address on lender applications immediately raises red flags about legitimacy.

iii

Mismatched business info

One typo across the IRS, your bank, and the Secretary of State = automatic denial.

iv

No operating agreement

Lenders ask for it. Without it, they assume you're not a real, structured entity.

v

No business bank account

Mixing personal and business finances destroys your funding chances before you start.

vi

Invisible online presence

No website. No 411 listing. No Google profile. To a lender, you're a ghost.

When lenders notice these gaps, they say "no."
Let's make sure that never happens to you.
